Our enterprise analytics platform was built incrementally over five years, resulting in an inconsistent user experience and declining user satisfaction. With growing competition and user complaints, we needed a comprehensive redesign.

The Challenge

Enterprise users were struggling with our analytics dashboard. The main pain points included:

  • Inconsistent UI patterns across different sections
  • Complex navigation making it hard to find specific data
  • Poor data visualization making insights unclear
  • Lack of customization options for different user roles
  • Slow load times and performance issues
"It takes me 15 minutes just to find the report I need. I often give up and export everything to Excel instead." — Enterprise Customer, Initial Research

Research & Discovery

I led a comprehensive research initiative to understand our users' needs and pain points:

Research Methods

User Interviews

Conducted 25+ interviews with enterprise customers across different industries

Analytics Analysis

Analyzed 6 months of usage data to identify patterns and bottlenecks

Competitive Analysis

Evaluated 8 competing platforms to understand industry standards

Usability Testing

Tested current platform with 15 users to document pain points

Key Insights

  1. Users needed personalization: Different roles required different dashboard views
  2. Visual hierarchy was missing: Everything looked equally important
  3. Performance matters: Slow load times led to task abandonment
  4. Mobile access was critical: 30% of users accessed on tablets

The Solution

Based on research insights, I designed a comprehensive solution focusing on personalization, clarity, and performance.

Key Learnings

1. Deep user research is non-negotiable

Spending time with actual users revealed pain points we never would have discovered through analytics alone. The personalization features came directly from observing different user workflows.

2. Incremental rollout reduces risk

We launched to 10% of users first, gathered feedback, and refined before full rollout. This caught several edge cases we hadn't considered.

3. Performance is a feature

Our focus on performance optimization was as important as any visual design decision. Fast load times dramatically improved user satisfaction.

4. Design systems enable speed

Building on our design system allowed us to iterate quickly while maintaining consistency across all dashboard components.
